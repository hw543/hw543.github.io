Aldemar drew his sword while Orin positioned himself protectively before the king. "What was that?" cried Orin. A warrior stationed at the kingdom's border rode into the city. His face was ashen, and his voice trembled as he called out: "The creatures from the stories are real! They're coming!" The Kingdom of Eldoria stood before its darkest hour, and the prophecy had begun to cast its shadows upon the land.

The Homecoming of the King and His Allies

Twilight bathed the towers of Eldoria in warm gold as Eryon and Aris rode through the cobbled streets of the city. People pressed close to the roadside, cheering and laughing. Eryon sat upon a majestic black horse, his golden armor sparkling in the light. A smile played on his lips, yet in his eyes lay a shadow that the crowd did not perceive. Beside him rode Aris, her hand firmly grasping the hilt of her sword. Her examining glances swept through the crowd, always on guard.

"The king has returned!" echoed a cry through the streets, and the crowd responded with a chorus of jubilation and songs. Yet among the exuberant faces, Aris noticed scattered, mistrustful looks.

Between the Lines of Festivity

Volume 3: The Shadows of the Forgotten

The morning sun bathed the vast plains of Eldoria in golden light, reflecting off the gentle hills. Kael sat at the edge of the cliff that overlooked the valley of Arien, letting his gaze sweep over the peaceful landscape. The scars of past wars seemed finally to be fading -- the fields bore rich harvests again, and the cities bloomed in new splendor. Yet in Kael's heart, unrest stirred, a fleeting longing for something greater that he could not name.

"You're thinking too much again," Tessa called behind him, her voice like a cheerful bell. She balanced a basket full of maps and scrolls in her hands and settled down beside him. "You know what that means."

Kael laughed softly and shook his head. "What does it mean, all-knowing Tessa?" "It means you're yearning for an adventure. And guess what -- I have just the right thing for us!" She laid the maps before her, corners carefully weighted with stones so the wind wouldn't carry them away. One map immediately caught the eye: Its lines were roughly drawn, details blurred, yet a red marking in a remote corner stood out.

"I found this in the archive of Eldarion," Tessa explained, her eyes sparkling with excitement. "It shows the western borders of Eldoria. Or rather, what we know about them -- namely, almost nothing." Kael furrowed his brow. "Those are the borderlands. No one goes there. The stories they tell..."

"Stories," she interrupted him, holding up a parchment scroll sealed with a royal seal. "And yet the realm sends us a message. This region is not as abandoned as we thought." Kael took the scroll and broke the seal. The words on it were brief and left an uneasy feeling:

To the scouts of Eldoria, A light has been sighted beyond the Mist Mountains. Its source is unknown, yet its power is unmistakable. The realm requests exploration -- and clarification.

Kael set the message aside and looked at Tessa, who waited with expectant eyes. "That sounds like trouble." "It sounds like an opportunity," she countered. "A chance to explore the unknown. Who knows what secrets lie hidden there?"

Kael felt the longing grow within him that had been driving him for weeks. Eldoria was safe, yet this safety left him restless. Perhaps this was the spark he had been seeking.

The villagers watched them with mixed feelings. Some shook their heads, others whispered anxiously about the dark myths of the borderlands.

"When we return," Tessa said with a wink, "I'll secure eternal glory for us. You'll see, Kael, in the future storytellers in every tavern will tell tales of us." Kael tightened the buckle of his backpack and cast a final glance at the village. "And if we don't return?" "Then at least we won't have died of boredom," she replied with a grin.

On the third day of their journey, they had left the familiar forests of Eldoria behind and reached the borders of the Mist Mountains. Here the air was different, heavy and full of eerie silence. The paths became narrower, and the fog thickened until they could barely see their hands before their eyes.

"We're almost there," Tessa murmured, matching the red cross on the map with their location. "At least I hope so." Suddenly a dull rumbling tore through the silence. The earth vibrated beneath their feet, and Kael instinctively drew his sword. "What was that?"

Tessa held her breath. Before them, the dense fog wall opened and revealed a glowing, pulsating light. It seemed to come directly from the ground and cast dancing shadows on the rocks. "The light," she whispered reverently. "It really exists."

But before they could take another step, a thunderous growl broke from the darkness, and two glowing eyes fixed on them. "Kael," Tessa breathed, "I think we're not alone."
