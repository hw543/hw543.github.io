Chapter 1: Closeness That Flees

Lena woke up with the feeling that something was missing. The warmth beside her was tangible, physically present, breathing, real. Julian's arm lay heavy across her middle, his breath steady and deep against her neck. And yet there existed a strange emptiness that she couldn't grasp. She blinked into the early morning light falling through the not quite closed curtains and tried to locate the sensation.

Three months had passed since the first kiss by the river. Three months in which hesitant touches had become familiarity, shared books had become a shared bed, two separate lives had become a common everyday life. Everything had developed so naturally, so effortlessly, as if it were a story that had only been waiting to be told.

Julian murmured something in his sleep, pulled her closer to him. She felt his heartbeat through the thin fabric of her T-shirt, soothing and familiar. She turned around and looked at Julian in his sleep. His hair fell into his forehead, his lips were slightly open. He looked younger in sleep, more vulnerable. Three months.
