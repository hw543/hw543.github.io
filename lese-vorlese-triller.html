Chapter 1: A New Life at the Fire Department

Lukas stood there, uncertain, with a hint of nervousness, while the flames flickered in sharp memory before his mind's eye. Since childhood, fire had exerted a strange fascination on him -- a powerful, all-consuming force that both attracted and intimidated him. In his harsh environment, where oppressive loneliness often surrounded him, fire was the only thing that seemed intense and alive to him. Here, at the fire department, Lukas believed he had finally found a way to tame this tremendous energy and direct it in a controlled, meaningful direction.

When he first entered the station, he felt the cool concrete floor under his new boots and breathed in the characteristic smell of smoke, metal, and motor oil, which seemed almost like a promise to him. The soundscape was busy yet calming; men and women who concentrated on preparing for their next deployment, checking tools and inspecting vehicles for emergencies. This is where he belonged, Lukas thought. A new beginning.

His colleagues briefly examined him, nodded to him, and went back to their work. No one knew his story, the emptiness that surrounded him, and the life he came from. Lukas had never been good at opening up to others or forming close friendships, but that didn't matter here. Here he wasn't judged by his past, but only by whether he kept his nerve in an emergency and was up to his tasks.

The first days passed quickly. Lukas learned how to operate the heavy equipment and completed grueling exercises. Every movement had to be perfect; precision and endurance were required. Soon he discovered a new kind of fulfillment that the work gave him. The routine, the hard training, and the discipline -- all of this gave him a sense of belonging and control that he had previously lacked. He finally felt like part of a community connected by a common purpose. But in quiet moments, when daylight fell through the station windows and the heat of a recently completed mission subsided, Lukas felt the flame of his inner passion flare up again. The fire wouldn't let him go; it drew him like a silent call under its spell.

One evening, after particularly demanding training, Lukas remained behind in the vehicle hall. The others had already left, and silence settled like a blanket over the empty emergency vehicles. He walked around, looked at the equipment, and let the impressions sink in. He knew that he had found a new direction in the fire department -- and yet there was something that still gnawed deep inside him. It was like a whisper that gained strength in the quiet moments between deployments. Lukas had hoped that his work as a firefighter would silence this desire that had accompanied him for years. But the more he got used to life at the fire department, the stronger became the inexplicable, burning desire to be very close to fire -- not just as a helper, but as someone who could actually control it.

With each deployment where flames were pushed back, Lukas felt an ambivalent mixture of pride and disappointment. Pride because he was part of the fire department that restored order and tamed chaos. But also disappointment because the fire always only affected him from the outside, while it pulsed deep within him in a way that no one understood -- he himself barely.

One night, when he tiredly and pensively made his way back to the fire station after a long day, he discovered smoldering embers at a small, abandoned hut on the outskirts of town. It was obvious that the fire had barely not broken out into an inferno, possibly extinguished early by chance. Lukas stopped, his gaze fixed on the glowing spot, and felt his heart begin to beat restlessly. His breathing became shallow as he stared into the deep darkness of the hut. He had an irresistible urge to touch the last embers, to feel how close he could be to fire without extinguishing it.

He could barely force himself to leave the place. It was as if an invisible bond tied him to the embers, whispered in his ear, and urged him to surrender completely to the fire. But with all his strength, he finally broke free from this strange paralysis and returned to the station. The thoughts of the flames that had been so close kept him awake all night.

The next morning, Lukas noticed that something had changed in him. The quiet fascination with fire, which had previously been only a subliminal part of his being, suddenly felt stronger, as if the glow of the hut had ignited it. During the next deployments, he seemed more tense than usual. His colleagues, who sensed the change in him, asked with concern if he was okay. Lukas assured them that everything was fine and tried to hide his inner restlessness. But internally he felt a power that was pulling him ever stronger.

In the following days, Lukas spent hours alone in the vehicle hall or with the equipment. He practiced movements, checked hoses and helmets over and over again -- but these activities weren't enough to satisfy the desire within him. His gaze constantly wandered to the fire trucks, which seemed so heavy and powerful, yet also exerted a frightening fascination on him, since they simultaneously offered the possibility of getting close to fire in a special way. It was as if he saw in them not just tools for fighting, but also keys that could open the doors to a dangerous, almost forbidden world.

A thought sprouted in him, as shy and furtive as a flame hiding under a pile of ashes. What if he could ignite a fire himself to see how it felt to observe a fire from close up that was completely under his control?

Lukas was startled by the thought, but it was there, like a small flame that slowly but steadily gained strength. He tried to suppress the impulse, to distract himself and concentrate on his tasks. But the idea wouldn't let him go. It crept back into his thoughts, especially in quiet moments when he was alone, and flickered like a blazing spark in the darkness of his mind.

He wondered how it would feel to have control over a fire that he had ignited himself. Part of him knew it was dangerous to pursue this thought further, but the longing for this feeling, for this power, grew with each day. It was like an urge he couldn't explain, but also couldn't ignore anymore. More and more often he caught himself mentally playing through situations: a small, safe fire that would only be limited to a small area, easy to control and quick to extinguish.

He spent evenings immersing himself in books and articles about fire causes and fire behavior. What had once begun as required reading for his work at the fire department had now become a secret obsession. Lukas wanted to know every detail: the chemical reactions, the right conditions for starting a fire, the different materials and their burning properties. He studied how flames spread, how they behaved in different environments, and especially how they could be controlled.

His knowledge, combined with his fascination, gave him the feeling of extraordinary power. But with this power also came inner conflict -- the voice of reason that spoke to his conscience and reminded him that as a firefighter he had sworn to extinguish fires and save lives, not endanger them.

Nevertheless, the idea grew in him. He began to imagine places, remote locations where he could light a small fire without putting anyone in danger. Remote meadows or old, abandoned buildings on the outskirts of town seemed ideal to him, secluded and far from prying eyes. The thought of the danger he would create himself gave him a strange feeling of vitality that he found nowhere else.

One evening, sitting alone in his room, he reached for the phone and began to compile a list of places that could be suitable. Each entry brought a mixture of nervousness and anticipation, as if he were guarding a secret that only he could understand.

But when he was on the verge of putting this plan into action, a touch of doubt returned. Was he really ready to cross this line? What if someone recognized the connection, if the other firefighters became suspicious? The thought of being discovered filled him with a dull fear, but also with an ominous tingling that almost drove him to continue.

In the end, he decided to postpone the decision, but the desire remained -- like a quiet, flickering flame that secretly spread within him, waiting only for the right moment to transform into an uncontrollable fire.
