The painstaking work that she and her colleagues performed in secret remained invisible. That was exactly how Elena wanted it. She preferred working in the background, immersing herself in the intimacy of artworks, learning their secrets. She gladly left the spotlight moments to others.

Schumann's office was located in the historic wing of the museum, a high room paneled with dark wood overlooking the museum park. When Elena entered after a brief knock, the director hastily rose from his desk.

Elena nodded briefly. The Castellano name was familiar to her, though only marginally. An old European family, art collectors for generations, their private museum legendary but notoriously difficult for the public to access.

"Yes, it is," she replied, quickly tucking the photo back among the other documents.

"And if I refuse?"

"Then we would have to think about budget cuts. Possibly in your department too." His voice was gentle, but the threat unmistakable.

Elena nodded slowly. She wasn't a politician, not a fighter in the administrative battles of the museum. Her world was colors and forms, chemical reactions and historical techniques -- not budgets and negotiations.

"I understand," she said curtly.

He left the office without waiting for her answer.

Elena gathered the documents together and slowly walked the long corridor back to her studio. On the way, she opened the folder again and pulled out the photo with the locked door. Why was it here? An oversight or a message?

She shook her head at her own thoughts. Too many old novels read, too many stories about mysterious art collectors heard. This was a professional offer, nothing more.

And yet... Marcus Castellano's eyes had seen something when he looked at her. Something that went beyond her professional abilities.

Arriving at her studio, Elena placed the folder on her desk and returned to Diana, whose face still patiently awaited liberation from centuries of false overpaintings.

"What would you do?" she murmured as she put her gloves back on. The goddess of the hunt remained silent, her eyes half-hidden under layers of foreign brushstrokes.

Elena picked up her scalpel again and began carefully working around the goddess's eye. Layer by layer she exposed, revealing the original beneath. As she always did -- methodically, patiently, controlled.

But her thoughts were already elsewhere. At a museum on an Italian cliff. With an enigmatic art collector with penetrating eyes. And at a locked door that seemed to be waiting for something that had long been forgotten.

When she left her studio that evening, she had already made her decision. She took out her phone and dialed the number Castellano had left her.

"Mr. Castellano," she said when he answered. "This is Elena Winters. I accept your offer."

A tiny pause arose, barely perceptible, but Elena noticed it nonetheless. "That will be arranged," Castellano said finally.

"And third, I reserve the right to terminate the project at any time if I find that the work does not meet my ethical or professional standards."

This time the pause was longer. "An interesting condition, Mrs. Winters. May I ask what concerns you have?"

Elena considered briefly. She didn't want to appear rude, but something about the whole situation made her cautious. "No specific concerns. Just professional care. I prefer clear agreements from the beginning."

"I respect that. Your conditions are accepted."

They discussed the practical details -- travel dates, necessary equipment, insurance questions. Elena noted everything in her precise, methodical style.

"I expect you then in a week," Castellano concluded. "My assistant will send you the travel documents."

"Until then, Mr. Castellano."

"See you soon, Mrs. Winters. And prepare yourself -- my collection will surprise you."

The connection was severed, and Elena was left with the strange feeling that she had just signed a contract whose fine print she didn't yet know.
